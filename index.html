About Me

My journey

After graduating high school, I was accepted into the School of Engineering at Virginia Commonwealth University where I pursued a degree in Mechanical Engineering. After 3 years, I chose to relocate to North Carolina to consider pursuing other passions. It was during this time that I found the game of poker. After just a few years, I believed my skills to be sharp enough to pursue poker full-time. I spent the next decade traveling the country and having a successful career.

I recently felt it was time for a career change, so I enrolled into App Academy's intense 24-week coding program. Their program has a less than 3% acceptance rate and approximately 1000 hours of software development training. It was a rigorous process of coding all hours of the day, much of which was spent working with others pairboarding or collaborating on projects.

Upon completion, I was able to build from scratch full stack applications (found in the "portfolio" section) using a variety of technologies (found in the "skills" section).

If you are in need of an eager-to-work full stack developer, feel free to send me an email or call me and I would love to talk.
